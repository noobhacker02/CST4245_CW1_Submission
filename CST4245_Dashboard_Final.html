Hypertension — Going Down

Global mean BP fell 5.65pp from 1980–2014, driven by antihypertensive medication access and salt-reduction campaigns.

⚠️
Obesity — Doubled in 35 Years

Global obesity doubled. Women consistently higher. Pacific Island nations at 59–65% — majority of adults clinically obese.

🇦🇪
UAE in the High-Risk Cluster

Female obesity 41.4%, diabetes 15.2%. Both place the UAE firmly in the Eastern Mediterranean high-risk cluster.

Chart 1 — Global Prevalence Trends (1980–2014)
Mean prevalence across 200 countries per year, split by sex. Independent y-axis per condition. Source: NCD-RisC.
Hypertension ↓ Men 31.9%→26.5%, Women 28.2%→22.3%. Men consistently higher throughout.
Obesity ↑↑ Women 11.2%→24.0%, Men 5.5%→16.7%. Sex gap widened. Female excess in Pacific Island and Gulf populations.
Diabetes ↑ Near-equal both sexes. Both roughly doubled from ~5% to ~10%.
Chart 2 — Hypertension Gender Gap by Region (Men − Women, %)
Regional mean of Male minus Female BP per year. Below zero = women higher. Source: NCD-RisC (2017).
Europe — most negative: Women 7–9pp higher than men throughout. Iceland, Estonia, Latvia, Czech Republic exceed −13pp by 2014.
Africa — only reversal: −2pp in 1980 to +0.24pp by 2014. Rising male risk and improving female treatment access.
Eastern Mediterranean: Started +0.37pp (men higher), crossed to women-higher around 1997 as Gulf female obesity rose sharply.
Chart 3 — Obesity by Country 2014: Top & Bottom 15
Stacked bars — blue = Men, red = Women stacked on top. X-axis = combined total. Source: NCD-RisC.
Top 15 — Pacific dominance: Nauru (59% M, 64% F), American Samoa, Cook Islands, Palau. Kuwait and Qatar represent Gulf cluster.
Bottom 15 — South/SE Asia + Africa: Vietnam (1.4% M, 2.4% F) at very bottom. Japan only high-income country here.
Chart 4 — Obesity vs Diabetes by Country (2014)
Each point = one country. X = mean obesity, Y = mean diabetes. Coloured by WHO region. Dashed = regression. UAE labelled.
Eastern Mediterranean above the line: More diabetes per unit obesity than predicted. Afghanistan: 5% obesity, 12% diabetes. South Asian and Arab populations develop diabetes at lower BMI.
East Asia bottom-left: Japan, South Korea, Vietnam — low obesity AND low diabetes. China notably further right from rapid urbanisation.
Chart 5 — Mean Hypertension by Region & Decade (%)
Mean BP averaged across both sexes and all countries per region per decade. Darker red = higher. Source: NCD-RisC.
Africa — flat: 28.6%–29.6% across all four decades. Only region that failed to improve. Low awareness, patchy screening.
Europe & Americas — biggest improvers: Europe 34.3%→25.1%, Americas 28.0%→21.7%. South-East Asia only region to worsen: 22.8%→24.7%.
Chart 6 — Global Diabetes Prevalence by Country (2014)
Both sexes mean diabetes. Orange-red scale — darker = higher. Grey = no match in topojson. Source: NCD-RisC (2016b).
Middle East — darkest cluster: Egypt, Iraq, Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar, Libya, UAE all above 15%. Geographically contiguous high-risk zone.
Sub-Saharan Africa — lightest: Mostly below 8%. Younger age structure, but projected to rise rapidly.
